# Video 2 — Repetition → escalation (the child's volume knob)

**Goal:** Show the single most distinctive idea in BlasterAI: a non-verbal child can't raise their voice, so they tap harder and more often — and BlasterAI reads that as rising urgency and escalates the language. This is also the clearest proof that there's *real AI judgment* here, which matters to Anthropic and OpenAI.
**Length:** 40–50s · **Orientation:** portrait · **Driver:** live taps (repeat the same tile) — easiest to control the repeat count.

## Notes for the editor
- **Escalate the visuals with the audio** — grow the tray text size and shift toward the coral accent on each repeat so muted viewers feel the crescendo.
- Keep the TTS real and let each spoken line land before the next tap. The plateau between taps 1 and 2 is intentional and true to the model — don't hide it; it makes the jump at tap 3 hit harder.
- The 0:40 credibility card is what makes this video land differently for the AI-lab audience. Keep it factual: *judge pass rate 38% → 85%, deterministic checks 0% → 100%.*
